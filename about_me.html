My name is Danielle Szaro. I grew up in the small (now quickly growing) town of Fuquay Varina,
North Carolina. I attended my schooling in Fuquay Varina before moving onto college in Wilmington,
North Carolina. I am a married mom of two energetic boys.

Growing up brought many challenges and school quickly became my safe place. My parents
divorced when I was young for reasons I was unsure of. I lived with my mother but would see my
father on the weekends. During my junior year of high school, my father was sentenced to 10 years in prison. He
had been on the wrong side of the legal system and had been living a double life I knew nothing about. As an
adolescent whose biggest worry was doing well at soccer practice and finishing a college application, my world
was completely turned upside down.

Luckily, I had the support of my teachers. They created a space that was open, honest, raw, and welcoming.
At home, my thoughts were scattered with worries of the future and what the sentencing meant for my
family moving forward. At school, however, I had a purpose. I had goals put before me to succeed in academics. I
was given books to take my mind to adventures elsewhere. With teacher support, I was able to get past the hurdle
of what had happened with my father and move forward with a new passion, education. Without their guidance
and compassion, I would not have found my place in the classroom as a teacher myself. I would not have realized
how important our differences can be and how they can thrive with a little push in the right direction.

As I have moved forward with my teaching career, I discovered new passions that can help our youth become the
best versions of themselves. One of those is the passion for reading instruction. As an adolescent, reading helped
me to not only learn but to escape reality to go anywhere I wanted. As a teacher, reading is of utmost importance
to my elementary students where information is at their fingertips constantly. Educators must teach students to
read and comprehend information in one school year. For that reason, the Science of Reading should be utilized.
